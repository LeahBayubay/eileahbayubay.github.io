What is Covid-19?
The novel CoronaVirus is generally referred to as COVID 19 and
was designated 2019-nCoV by the World Health Organization (WHO).
This virus is now referred to as Severe Acute Respiratory
Syndrome Coronavirus 2 ( SARS-CoV-2 ). It is responsible for the latest
virus outbreak that started at the start of December 2019. The first reported
case was in Wuhan City, Hubei Province in China, which in many cases
led to severe pneumonia and was exported to a number of countries and
cities across the globe.
Coronavirus disease, also known as COVID19 is an infectious
disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus. It is a large family
of viruses that cause illness ranging from the common cold to more severe
diseases. A novel coronavirus (nCoV) is a new strain that has not
been previously identified in humans.
